# Crafting software solutions with a focus on the cloud

In recent years my primary professional focus has been Go, complemented by substantial experience with Elixir in commercial projects.

My current stack includes:
- **Go**
- **Nomad**
- **AWS**

I’ve worked professionally with Java and Ruby, and more recently with Rails in a recent project. I’ve also worked with Clojure and ClojureScript, including re-frame, which I found exquisite. These experiences, not all long but the most recent one fairly fresh, reinforced my appreciation for functional design. Over time I’ve followed my curiosity into different languages that shaped how I think about software: Elm, for its functional purity; Dart, which I used in small apps and found pragmatic and clear (and the best way to avoid diving too deep into JavaScript when the need arose, though Flutter, while good, tends to give Dart the feel of writing structured configuration rather than traditional code); and Roc, which I see as carrying Elm’s influence into systems and tooling. I also spend time with Racket, my personal equivalent to crosswords, a place to experiment with alternative paradigms even if it has little traction in the professional world. More recently I have been exploring Gleam, drawn by the way it brings static typing to the BEAM ecosystem I already value. I would not call myself an expert in these languages, more of a beginner following threads of curiosity, but this is one I would like to grow deeper into over time.

I am especially drawn to DDD, distributed systems, and writing about software design. Outside of work I build self-hosted tools that solve focused problems, a way to stay close to the craft while balancing large-scale infrastructure work.
